Goodreads Choice Awards 2023

By The Numbers

By Natalie Weger

Millions of readers across the world flock to their screens each year to vote for their favorite books in the Goodreads Choice Awards. The online voting on dozens of books is run by Goodreads — the world’s largest website for readers and book recommendations.

Anyone with a Goodreads account can participate in the Choice Awards. The voting separates books into several categories — including fiction, fantasy and historical fiction — and allows readers to vote on their favorites in each category.

Here are the 2023 choice awards, by the numbers.

Some of the most popular books in 2023, as voted on by GoodReads readers, included fan favorites such as “Divine Rivals” by Rebecca Ross, “Happy Place” by Emily Henry and “In the Lives of Puppets” by TJ Klune.

This year’s choice awards also saw some familiar authors, including Stephen King and Leigh Bardugo. “Weyward” by Emila Hart saw two wins this year in the debut novel and historical fiction category.

However, the book that won its category by the largest landslide in 2023 was “Fourth Wing” by Rebecca Yaros. This fantasy book, which debuted in May, features the journey of a 20-year-old girl as she joins hundreds of candidates to become an elite rider in a dragon-riding school. The book is a blend of war, dragons, magic, romance, enemies and overcoming adversity.

“Fourth Wing” has sold more than 2 million copies globally and has appeared on the New York Times bestseller list for more than six months. The saga continues in Yaros’ second novel, “Iron Flame.”

This book has dominated its romantasy category with 397,565 votes — meaning that the novel made up almost 70 percent of votes in its category. The runner-up for the romantasy category was “Assistant To The Villain” by Hannah Nicole Maehrer.

The next two books that saw the biggest percentage of votes in their categories were “Yellowface” by R.F. Kuang and “The Woman in Me” by Britney Spears. Both of these books took about 34 percent of votes in their respective categories of fiction and memoir.

There were 15 categories that were voted on by readers in 2023, ranging from humor to horror. Although readers were not required to vote on every single category, the amount of votes cast for each category reflects the total popularity of different types of books.

In 2023, the most popular category voted on was romance with 645,601 votes. This category had notable reads such as “Love, Theoretically” by Ali Hazelwood and “Yours Truly” by Abby Jimenez.

Fiction became the second most popular category voted on, which saw books such as “Hello Beautiful” by Ann Napolitano and “The Wishing Game” by Meg Shaffer.

The dozens of books that the Goodreads Choice Awards saw in 2023 are among hundreds that have been voted on by readers for more than a decade. The Choice Awards first started in 2011, when it saw 2010s classics such as “Divergent” by Veronica Roth and “The Son of Neptune” by Rick Riordan.

Now, voting on the GoodReads choice awards have increased by almost eight times from 2023 to 2011. This happened as GoodReads gained more popularity and trends such as BookTok increased book engagement throughout the years.

The next GoodReads choice awards will most likely open around November 2024. Until then, readers across the world will keep on digging into their favorite books.
